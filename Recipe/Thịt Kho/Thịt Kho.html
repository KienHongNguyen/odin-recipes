Thịt Kho

Ingredients

  • 500g porks belly
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • 2 tablespoon sugar (1 for pork, 1 for caramel)
  • 2 tablespoon fish sauce (1 for marinate, 1 for sim)
  • 2 cloves garlic
  • 1/4 onion
  • 1 tablespoon oil
  • 1 coconut water

Steps

  1. Cut the Pork: Using a sharp knife, cut 500g of pork belly or shoulder into 2-3 inch cubes or chunks.
  2. Marinate the Pork: Place the cut pork pieces in a bowl. Add 1/2 teaspoon salt, 1 tablespoon brown sugar, and 1 tablespoon fish sauce. Use your hands or a spoon to toss and coat the pork evenly with the marinade. Let it marinate for 15-30 minutes.
  3. Prepare Aromatics: Peel and thinly slice 2 cloves of garlic. Also thinly slice 1/4 of a medium onion bulb.
  4. Cook Aromatics: In a heavy bottomed pan or pot, heat 2 tablespoons of vegetable or olive oil over medium-high heat. Once hot, add the sliced garlic and onion. Cook for 1-2 minutes, stirring frequently, until fragrant and lightly browned.
  5. Cook the Pork: Remove the pork pieces from the marinade, reserving the liquid. Add the marinated pork pieces to the pan with the cooked aromatics. Cook for 2-3 minutes undisturbed to get a nice sear. Then continue cooking for 2-3 more minutes, turning occasionally until the pork is browned on all sides. Remove from heat.
  6. Make the Caramel: In a separate small pot or saucepan, add 1 tablespoon of white granulated sugar. Cook over medium heat, constantly swirling the pan, until the sugar fully melts and turns an amber caramel color, about 5 minutes.
  7. Caramelize Evenly: Once a nice amber caramel forms, continue swirling the pot so the caramel coats the bottom and sides evenly. Allow to cook for 1-2 more minutes until it deepens to a reddish-brown color, being careful not to burn.
  8. Add Coconut Water and Pork: Working carefully as the caramel is very hot, slowly add about 1-1.5 cups of coconut water to the caramel pot. Stir and scrape up any caramel bits from the bottom. Then add the browned pork and juices.
  9. Simmer the Pork: Add enough water to the caramel pot to just cover the top of the pork pieces, about 1-1.5 cups. Bring to a gentle simmer over medium-low heat. Simmer for 45-60 minutes, adjusting heat as needed to maintain a low simmer, until pork is very tender and the braising liquid has thickened into a glaze, stirring occasionally.
  10. Finish and Serve: Once pork is fork-tender, taste and adjust seasoning with more fish sauce, sugar or salt if needed. Transfer the braised caramelized pork (Thịt Kho) along with the sauce to a serving dish. Garnish with sliced green onions or chilies if desired. Serve hot over steamed jasmine rice.
