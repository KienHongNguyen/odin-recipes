Flan

Ingredients

  • 6 eggs
  • 180g of sugar (30g per egg)
  • 600ml of milk (100ml per egg)
  • 150g of sugar for caramel
  • 60ml of water for caramel

Steps

  1. Make the Caramel: In a saucepan, combine 150g sugar and 60ml water. Heat over medium, stirring occasionally, until the sugar dissolves and turns into an amber-colored caramel, about 10 minutes. Immediately pour the caramel into a round baking pan or ramekins and tilt to coat the bottoms and sides. Set aside to cool.
  2. Prepare the Egg Mixture: Crack 6 eggs into a large bowl and whisk until fully combined.
  3. Heat the Milk: In a saucepan, heat 600ml milk over low heat. Add 180g white sugar and whisk continuously until the 180g sugar fully dissolves.
  4. Temper the Eggs: Slowly pour the warm milk mixture into the beaten eggs while whisking constantly to temper the eggs.
  5. Strain the Custard: Use a fine mesh strainer to strain the egg-milk mixture into a clean bowl or pitcher to remove any bits or foam.
  6. Fill the Ramekins: Slowly pour the strained custard over the caramelized sugar in the baking pan or ramekins.
  7. Prepare the Water Bath: Place the filled ramekins into a large baking pan or dish and pour hot water around them until it reaches about 1/4 up the sides.
  8. Bake the Flan: Preheat oven to 120Â°C. Carefully transfer the water bath to the oven. Bake for 45 minutes, checking every 15 minutes, until the flan is set but still jiggles slightly in the center.
  9. Cool and Chill: Remove from oven and water bath and let cool completely. Then refrigerate for at least 2 hours before serving.
  10. Unmold and Serve: To serve, run a knife carefully around the edges of the ramekins. Invert onto plates, allowing the caramel sauce to drizzle over the top.
